## How InterGen CEOs Are Using AI
*Output from discussion — ~30 distinct uses clustered into 7 themes*

**Key pattern across all themes:** A shift from treating AI as a *tool* to treating it as a *team member*, with a strong build-and-iterate mindset over buying off-the-shelf.

### 1. Agentic Systems & Delegation
Building autonomous agents to offload work, with appropriate controls. Examples: executive-assistant agents with read-only access to email, GitHub, and Teams; agents for finance and personal tasks; defined "environments" and "contracts" with agents as guardrails.

### 2. Prototyping, Testing & Sandboxes
Safe spaces to experiment and iterate quickly. Examples: a website/sandbox for the team to test ideas; tying a sandbox to Claude for in-line builds, ideation, and mockups; creating prototypes for iteration; a pricing model to stress-test scenarios.

### 3. Sales, Business Development & Client Work
Revenue-facing applications. Examples: transcribing and scoring customer discussions for sales training; developing BD strategy; proposals and pitch decks; client summaries; grant writing.

### 4. Hiring & Talent Development
People-related uses. Examples: scorecards and role criteria for hiring; getting AI to take on roles or personas; standardizing coding practices; training and testing interns.

### 5. Knowledge Work & Thinking Partner
AI as a research and ideation collaborator. Examples: reading papers and asking questions; serving as memory; a partner to talk to and clarify ideas; brainstorming a future vision; testing hypotheses; mind-maps.

### 6. Content Generation & Creative Production
Producing polished outputs. Examples: report and product generation; NotebookLM to reformat content and create podcasts; design review; style guides via Claude.

### 7. Operations & Admin Automation
Day-to-day efficiency. Examples: drafting emails and general admin; document and contract generation.

## Delegation Framework — What to Let Go Of
*InterGen Peer Forum Discussion Output, May 2026*

1. **Delegate Everything** — Promote yourself into the role that you need to be acting in to best serve the company and the people.
2. **Work as Hard as Everybody Else** — Delegating doesn't mean not working. Keep your finger on the pulse so you know when and where to do a deep dive. Because you've delegated the non-essentials, you have the capacity to dig in when required.
3. **Create Systems to Monitor** — You can't do everything, but you still need to know what is going on. A cadence of reporting and check-ins keeps everybody working in the same direction.
4. **Invest in People and Their Growth** — Delegation is a process. It takes investment of time and resources to get people ready to take on more.
5. **Ensure Clarity and Set a High Bar** — It may require several iterations to get people to where they need to be.
6. **Don't Be Afraid to Say Goodbye** — Even when you do everything right, some people are not able to deliver what the company needs. After reflecting on whether the right steps have been taken, the best path forward (for everybody) is to remove them from that position.
