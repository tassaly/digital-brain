# From War Damage to Rapid Reconstruction
**How Digital Recommerce Platforms Can Overcome OEM Bottlenecks on Oil & Gas Equipment**
*Peter C. Evans, May 13, 2026*

## Why This Matters to IronHub
This report explicitly names IronHub as a proven model for surplus equipment recommerce, and makes the case that platforms like IronHub are the solution to a $34–58B reconstruction crisis in the Middle East. It is a powerful third-party validation of IronHub's value proposition and global expansion opportunity.

## Key Facts & Stats

### The Crisis
- War with Iran (started Feb 28, 2026) damaged 80+ oil & gas facilities
- Total infrastructure damage: **$34B–$58B** (Rystad Energy)
- Qatar's Ras Laffan LNG: **17% of global LNG offline**, $20B/yr in lost income
- Iran repair costs: ~$19B
- Recovery constrained by **equipment lead times of 9 months to 5 years**

### OEM Lead Times (New Equipment)
| Equipment | New OEM Lead Time |
|-----------|------------------|
| Large frame gas turbines | 4–6 years (260 weeks) |
| Small frame gas turbines | 20–36 months |
| Centrifugal compressors | 18–36 months |
| Process reactors | 18–30 months |
| Heat exchangers | 12–24 months |
| Separators | 6–12 months |

OEMs booked solid through 2029–2030 due to global data center demand + reconstruction.

### Surplus Equipment Advantage
- Used/surplus is **84–92% faster** across all categories
- Cost savings of **30–70%** vs new OEM
- Large frame turbine: 260 weeks → 52 weeks (80% faster)
- Small frame turbine: 156 weeks → 20 weeks (87% faster)
- Heat exchangers: 78 weeks → 6 weeks (92% faster)

### Scale of Available Surplus
- IronHub analysis of top 30 Canadian upstream producers: **~3% of PP&E listed as surplus = $4.8B**
- Including undocumented surplus: **~7% = $22.4B in Canada alone**
- Canada = 5.5% of global upstream O&G
- **Global surplus (book value): $87B–$407B**
- **Global surplus (fair market value): $1.2T–$2.0T**
- Problem is not equipment shortage — it's **finding and accessing it**

## Three Sourcing Pathways for IOCs/NOCs/EPCs

### Pathway 1: Internal Sourcing
Use your own idle assets via SAP EAM, Oracle, or internal marketplaces. Fastest option — days vs. months.

### Pathway 2: Peer-to-Peer
Trade surplus between operators. One operator's idle compressor = another's urgent need.

### Pathway 3: EPC & Third-Party (IronHub's lane)
Platforms aggregate global surplus from auctions, liquidations, brokers. Average sourcing time: **104 weeks → under 20 weeks**.

## Three Barriers to Adoption
1. **Visibility Gaps** — surplus sits in spreadsheets, old ERPs, unlogged yards
2. **Data Standardization** — no common taxonomy across operators
3. **EPC Adoption** — default to OEM due to liability/vendor lists

## Canada as Proof of Concept
- IronHub cited directly as the working model
- Canadian platforms have proven: aggregation, standardization, and verification at scale works
- Equipment listed on platform moves to active projects within **weeks**
- Key insight: *"The main constraint is not availability of equipment — it is the industry's ability to find it, trust it, and move it quickly."*

## Conclusion / IronHub Opportunity
The report's call to action is for IOCs, NOCs, and EPCs to adopt platforms like IronHub globally, **now**, before new OEM orders become the only option. IronHub's Canadian model is positioned as the template for a globally-connected recommerce infrastructure.
