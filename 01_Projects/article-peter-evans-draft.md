# From War Damage to Rapid Reconstruction: How Digital Recommerce Platforms Can Overcome OEM Bottlenecks on Oil & Gas Equipment

**Authors:** Peter C. Evans and Taylor Assaly
**Status:** Published — May 13, 2026 on Substack
**Substack Link:** https://platformprofessional.substack.com/p/d25be4db
**Co-author Invite:** Accepted
**Promotion:** LinkedIn posts live May 14; coordinating with Geoffrey for May 21 distribution push

## Summary
The article discusses the severe damage to Middle East oil and gas infrastructure following the war with Iran (starting Feb 2026) and the challenges of rebuilding due to long lead times for new OEM equipment (up to 5 years). It argues that digital recommerce platforms (like IronHub) are the solution, as they can source surplus and used equipment 84-92% faster and 30-70% cheaper.

## Key Points
- **Damage:** $34B - $58B in damage, over 80 facilities affected (Qatar, Iran, UAE, Saudi Arabia, Kuwait).
- **OEM Bottlenecks:** Large frame gas turbines take 5 years (260 weeks). Centrifugal compressors take over 2 years.
- **The Recommerce Solution:** Used/surplus equipment cuts lead times drastically (e.g., turbines from 260 weeks to 52 weeks).
- **Cost Savings:** 30-70% cheaper than new OEM prices.
- **Available Surplus:** IronHub analysis shows massive global idle inventory (estimated $280B to $350B at book value, potentially over $1T at market value).
- **Three Sourcing Pathways:**
  1. Internal Sourcing (using own idle assets)
  2. Peer-to-Peer Sharing (buying from other operators)
  3. EPC and Third-Party Sourcing (global auctions/brokers)
- **Challenges to Overcome:** Limited visibility, lack of data standardization, and EPC adoption barriers (cultural preference for new equipment).
